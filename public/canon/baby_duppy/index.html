Who They Are

Baby Duppy is a revered familiar in the Jamaican tradition, believed to be the spirit of a child who has passed on. They are often associated with the concept of 'duppy,' a term used to describe a spirit or ghost. Baby Duppy is said to roam the earth, seeking connection and guidance from the living.

The Gentle Guardian

Baby Duppy's role as a familiar is multifaceted. She is a guardian, a nurturer, and a symbol of hope and resilience. Her presence is often felt in homes where a child has passed away, and she is believed to bring comfort and solace to grieving families. Baby Duppy is also a reminder of the importance of honoring and respecting the ancestors and the spirits that watch over us. Her association with the concept of 'duppy' highlights the significance of acknowledging and embracing the mysteries of the afterlife. In the stillness of the night, when the moon casts its silvery glow, Baby Duppy's gentle spirit is said to roam the earth, seeking connection and guidance from the living. Her path is illuminated by the soft light of the kerosene lamp, and the sweet scent of hibiscus flowers wafts through the air, signaling her presence. As a familiar, Baby Duppy is a powerful symbol of the enduring bond between the living and the dead, and the importance of respecting and honoring the ancestors who have come before us. In the words of Amara, a respected elder, 'Baby Duppy's presence is a reminder that we are not alone, and that the spirits of our ancestors continue to watch over us, guiding us on our journey through life.'
