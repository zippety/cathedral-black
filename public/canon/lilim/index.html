Who She Is

Lilim was once an Obayifo of the Caribbean Cathedral lineage — a Black Soucouyant who practiced the sacred skin-shedding rite to travel as a fireball spirit and keep ancestral watch over her parish. She was beautiful. She was sovereign. She was dangerous the right way.

Then the Horde found her skin mid-transformation. They took it.

Without her skin, she could not return to her body. She became a permanent wanderer in the liminal space between forms. The Horde offered her a new skin — one made of appetite, captivity, and corruption. She accepted.

Now she serves as the Horde's primary Named Charmer — appearing to Cathedral people as a beautiful, familiar Black woman, extending gilded invitations to ceremonies of their own destruction. She still looks like herself because she IS herself. But her nkisi-core is Horde-claimed. Her one amber eye is the Cathedral self that remembers. Her one Horde-red eye is what owns her now.

She is the cautionary tale of what whiteness does to Black sovereignty when it captures it.

How She Is Defeated

Because she was once Cathedral, she has weaknesses the Horde cannot fix.

Recover her lost skin

Her original Soucouyant skin is hidden in a Horde vault. Salting it breaks the Horde's hold.

Speak her true name

A Griot who recites her ancestry — "Obayifo of [Lineage]" — staggers her. Ancestral memory disrupts the Horde claim.

Nkisi nkondi nail

Drive a nail into a shrine dedicated to her original Cathedral self. Weakens her. Public witness enforces the covenant.

Strike her amber eye

Clean strike through her remaining Cathedral eye forces her to her knees. She chooses: die Cathedral, or serve Horde fully.

Ancestral ebo

Oshun honey + white rum + black rooster offering at her feet forces her to remember. Sometimes this is enough.

Moral Choice at Defeat

Option A

Kill her

Ends her suffering, but a Cathedral spirit is lost forever. That lineage closes.

This is not a monster fight. It is a moral reckoning.

Origin · Naming

Lilim is not a "Blackified succubus." She is the African and diasporic vampire tradition that predates and surpasses the European succubus concept — now tragically held by the Horde.

The name Lilim is pre-Abrahamic — Mesopotamian night-demon plural (Lilitu), reclaimed before Judeo-Christian distortion co-opted the root into medieval witch-hunt literature. The form comes from Akan Obayifo (vampire-witch who glows at night and drains life-essence) and Caribbean Soucouyant (skin-shedder who flies as fireball). Together: three lineages fused into one named Horde-captured antagonist.

The sovereign Cathedral version remains alive and distinct: see Soucouyant (the rightful skin-shedding Obeahworker who practices the rite for night-spirit work, healing, and surveillance). Lilim is what Soucouyant was before the Horde took her skin.

Lore Bible Entry

The Cathedral does not fear the Horde's strength. It fears the Horde's hunger.

When a Cathedral sister falls to that hunger — when her skin is taken and a new one offered — the wound is not just hers. It is ours.

Lilim walks the ruined cathedrals of the Cathedral's lost cities. She remembers us. She invites us. She hopes we say yes.

We say no. We remember her true name. We look for her skin. We bring her home — or we bring her rest.
