Who She Is

The Soucouyant is the sovereign Cathedral form of the blood-drinker lineage — a Black Caribbean elder woman who has earned the right to shed her skin and fly as a ball of gold flame through the night sky. She is not a monster. She is an institution.

By day she is respected in her village — a healer, a midwife, a reader of signs. Children run errands for her. Mothers bring sick babies to her doorstep. She accepts payment in herbs, fish, quiet gratitude.

By night — after the village is asleep and her skin is hung folded on the low branch of the silk-cotton tree like a ceremonial robe — she becomes the gold-flame patroller. She drinks only from those who would harm her people: Horde predators, Mandate drones, slave-catchers in her memory, any creature of appetite that crosses her line.

She is the older, truer, sovereign form of what Europe would later call "vampire." The aristocrat-predator of that lineage is the corruption. The Soucouyant is the root.

Two Forms

Daylight · The Healer

Elder Healer Form

Afro-Caribbean elder in her 60s–70s. Blue Madras wrap, white head-tie, coral beads. Tends a cast-iron pot of medicinal herbs — soursop, bitter bush, vervine, ginger root. Calm, knowing, unafraid. The mother at the door does not fear her — she brought her child here. This is the form the Cathedral sees.

Night · The Patroller

Gold-Flame Hunter Form

Fully Ital. Gold-Adinkra keloid sigils glow across bare skin — real aged body, natural proportions, sovereign elder. She takes flight as a comet of gold fire (not sickly green — that is Horde-corruption, not Cathedral root). Hunts Horde predators only. Returns before sunrise to her skin-mortar. This is the form the Horde dies to.

Folk Weaknesses

The Soucouyant has her limits. The same folk traditions that recognize her power also preserve the ways to stop one who has turned wrong — or who has been Horde-captured (see: Lilim).

Salt the skin

If her folded skin is rubbed with salt while she flies, she cannot return to it. At sunrise she burns. The oldest and surest weakness.

Rice at the threshold

Scatter rice grains at a doorway she is trying to cross. She must count every grain. Sunrise often catches her mid-count.

Witness-speak her name

If her full ancestral name is spoken by a Griot or Mambo in public witness, the binding between her two forms trembles. Sometimes this is enough to bring her home.

Ancestral ebo

Offerings of honey, white rum, and a white fowl at the silk-cotton tree where her skin rests remind her which side she is on.

Sunrise itself

She is a night patroller. Dawn finds her vulnerable. If she is caught outside her skin at sunrise, she burns.

Kinship to Lilim

Sovereign root ↔ Horde-captured variant

She is what Lilim was.

The Lilim you read about on the Horde canon page was once a Soucouyant. Her skin was taken mid-transformation. She could not return to her body. The Horde offered her a new skin made of appetite and corruption — she accepted.

Every Soucouyant is one stolen-skin away from becoming Lilim. Every Lilim is one recovered-skin away from becoming Soucouyant again.

This is why the Cathedral guards the silk-cotton trees of Trinidad like cathedrals themselves. Because the skin resting in the mortar at the roots is not just cloth. It is sovereignty.
